Undergraduate Research Projects

Hand Input Tap Classification


Imagine your body as a personal computer. Check emails and take calls by pressing the virtual buttons on your hand. This is the future, but it's happening sooner than you think!

Biologically-inspired Water Strider Robot


Water striders are interesting because of their agility on water. They have the ability to move, jump, and land, all with apparent ease. In our robotic prototype, we mimic the strider's use of surface tension and present a concept for jumping.

Spectral Graph Theory and Big Data


Big data sets that can be represented as graphs have grown to be arbitrarily large, particularly in social networks. We want to better quantify these relationships and apply the information to machine learning. If Alice studies for 6 hours a day and Bob studies for 3 hours a day, can we make any predictions as to how many hours a day Carol studies?

Flux-gate Magnetometer Sensor


It's important to know where your enemies are during a battle. In a desert environment, sand and wind can obscure soldiers' view. Alternatively, by sensing disturbances in the Earth's magnetic field, ground troops can determine where enemy tanks are located.

Temperature-Dependent Raman Spectroscopy of Fullerene Nanocar Wheels


Drug delivery is just as important as taking the right type of drug, be it ingestion or injection. Nanocars, molecular-sized vehicles whose movement can be controlled via temperature, are a promising way to better control delivery to a desired location.

High School Research Projects

CO2 Storage in Novel CO2-H2O Phases at High Pressure


Global warming threatens to disrupt our environment's natural cycle, and the gradual destruction of the ozone can be attributed in part to carbon dioxide. In this project, we experiment with the storage of carbon dioxide in a molecular cage formed by water under high pressures.

Mobile Robot Communication


With the advent of swarm robotics and distributed computing, the ability for robots to autonomously divide-and-conquer tasks has also increased in significance. I experimented with how two robots can be programmed to map out a room using sonar.

Nail It


Construction tools can be dangerous. As part of our high school safety project, we conceptualized and prototyped a device that would make it safer to use a hammer and nail with less risk to the user's fingers.
